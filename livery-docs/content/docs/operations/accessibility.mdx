---
title: Accessibility
description: Make generated technical visuals understandable without pointer, motion, or sight.
---

SVG exports include an accessible title and description derived from the figure and solved scene. Give figures meaningful titles and keep labels informative rather than decorative.

## Application checklist

- Provide surrounding prose for conclusions the geometry alone cannot communicate.
- Do not encode status only with color; use labels, connector variants, or semantic structure.
- Test theme contrast and product-level forced-color behavior.
- Keep labels readable at every supported width.
- Preserve visible keyboard focus around timeline controls.
- Announce selected timeline state and support arrow, Home, and End keys.
- Honor `prefers-reduced-motion`; Livery timeline animation already checks it.
- Provide a textual or tabular alternative for a dense visual when the relationships are essential.

The renderer cannot make hostile or meaningless model labels accessible. Validate generated content and the surrounding explanation before presentation.

Canvas primitives require deliberate text alternatives. A complex path without a useful figure title or nearby explanation remains opaque even when its SVG is technically valid.
